Read Me — Passes, Discounts &amp; Notes
Reciprocal membership networks - One membership unlocks free or discounted entry at hundreds of others. Some single memberships belong to MULTIPLE networks - so pick your most-visited local spot, then check which networks its family membership includes.
ASTC Passport (science centers): FREE general admission - but excludes museums within 90 mi of the one you join. So join a cheap far-away one to cover Boston Museum of Science / EcoTarium / Montshire when you travel.
ACM (children's museums): 50% off, up to 6 people, at ~200 children's museums.
AZA (zoos/aquariums): free or 50% off at many zoos and aquariums nationwide.
NARM (1,000+ museums/gardens/zoos): full free entry; join a NARM museum (often ~$100-125 at the family level).
OSL library passes (Rochambeau): Reserve through CLPVD museum-pass app; pick up at Rochambeau. Any OSL cardholder can use any branch's pass. You have to reserve and physically check out the pass (limited copies, books up fast), it's one pass per institution, and many have blackout days. So &quot;free&quot; really means &quot;free admission for your family if you snag the pass for an eligible day&quot; — not free anytime you walk up.
FREE with the OSL pass:
Audubon Nature Center &amp; Aquarium — free for 6 people (and the trails are always free anyway; under 4 always free)
Capron Park Zoo — free for up to 4 guests (note: the pass requires North Providence or Rogers Free–type residency at some branches)
Buttonwood Park Zoo — free for up to 4 people, but not valid Mondays, Saturdays, holidays, or Feb/April school vacation weeks
Herreshoff Marine Museum — free for 2 adults + all their children (seasonal pass)
Norman Bird Sanctuary — free for up to 6 people
RISD Museum — free for 2 adults, and kids are always free regardless
Smith&#x2019;s Castle – free for 2 adults + 3 children on a regularly scheulded tour, May-Oct
